Reflections on Mindfullness and Work

John Kabait Zihn describes mindfulness as the simple act of paying attention on purpose.

I like to think about this as turning on a switch.

The off-mode is the mindless state.

The on-mode is when you're in a mindful state.

However, there's an important caveat in this switch analogy. This is a unique switch. Unlike most switches, this switch has a tension, rubber band, that slowly pulls it back to turning off - entering a mindless state.

oftentime, the switch actually feels most comfortable being off. So it will try to pull itself down and turn off on its own. Sometimes, it's challenging to notice when it inadvertently switches off. When this happens, it's important to notice when it does turn off and try to turn it back on by simply attempting to purposefully pay attention.

I've found that for tasks requiring deep concentration, it's helpful to have fun and enjoy what you're doing. that sounds silly and simply but having fun I believe is really crucial for work and creativity. If I'm not finding enjoyment in my work, the temptation to disengage and enter a mindless state becomes stronger, making it harder to stay mindful. Sometimes, while working, if I notice my focus fading, I'll reflect on what I enjoy about the task, what excites me, or why it's important.

However, after prolonged concentration, I can feel tension building in my body, and I might lose awareness, slipping into a mindless state. There are usually physical signs that I may or may not notice: my leg starts bouncing, I feel restless, and I have the urge to move. When I realize that my focus has lapsed, I try to take a deep breath, becoming aware of my body, and consciously re-engaging helps me refocus.

For me, Rediscovering the fun in the task is crucial to this process.
