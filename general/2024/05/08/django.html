The Django Journey

One of the beautiful things about life is you can spend countless hours doing anything you want and you can take up any tedious + silly challenge. Some people do crazy insanse things likefree climb a 90 degree slab of rock - like El Capitan in yosemite and risk their lives in the process.

I'm taking up my own pointless challenge. I'm learning Django and backend programming to build web applications. It's definitely a challenge. And it can feel tedious at times. there's a chance its a pointless pursuit - there's always the very real possibility lurking in the background that tools like AI will make programming skills obsolete and pointless.

Programming is like trying to complete a very long and detailed Lego set or setting up an incredibly complex piece of furniture, you're essentially just following or writing detailed directions, one after another.

Do I enjoy it ? Am I having fun ? I'm not entirely sure yet. I'm in the trenches. But along the way, I want to archive the journey.
